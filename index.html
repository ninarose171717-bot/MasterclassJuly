You do everything right.

You show up the way you should.

You have people who love you.

You've built a beautiful life.

Maybe it's even the life you once dreamed of.

And yet.

  • You feel overwhelmed.
  • You keep looking forward to the next thing.
  • You struggle to fully relax.
  • You know something doesn't feel quite right, but you've learned to ignore it.

And somewhere along the way, you've completely stopped asking for what you want.

You keep thinking:

"When things calm down …"
"When I finally figure this out …"
"When I get there …"

Then I'll feel better. Then I'll feel lighter. Then I'll feel like myself again.

But somehow, that moment never comes.

Somehow there's always something else,
that needs your attention more than you.

Somehow you can just never come up with an excuse to take care of you.

But what if it has nothing to do with
how much you have on your plate?

What if it's not your partner, your job, your circumstances?

What if there's something else going on, something most of us were never taught to see?

In this free masterclass, I'll share the shift that changed everything for me.

You probably have great relationships with everyone else,

But how you relate to yourself shapes everything.
Your relationships, your desires, your decisions.

The way your days actually feel.

About Me

I've spent the last three years working with women, online and in person, around one pattern that sits beneath so much of what keeps us stuck:
the habit of leaving ourselves.

I also work in rehab with people in recovery.
Different circumstances, same question:
how do we stay with ourselves when life becomes difficult?

I'm a somatic and breathwork practitioner, trained in parts work.
My work is grounded in the body, because real change doesn't happen through insight alone.

But more than any method or modality, I bring a willingness to be there for ourselves in the hard moments, without rushing to fix, change, numb,
or look away.

I'm originally from Slovenia and now live in Thailand.
Choosing a life that felt true rather than one that looked right has shaped everything about the work I do.

For years, I believed that if I could just heal enough, understand myself enough, figure out the right thing, I would finally feel at peace.

But it never lasted. Instead, I found myself reaching for everything outside of me to fill something I couldn't name.

I chased men who couldn't show up for me, drank every day just to cope with my anxiety, and the only time I could actually open up was when I was on something.

I kept looking for safety in everyone and everything but myself.

I tried so many things to get better, and I wanted all of them to work at the same time.

Nothing really worked. Until I learned, slowly, gently, and with a lot of love, how to actually be with myself. Not only my emotions. Myself.

And somewhere in that process, I stopped needing what I was reaching for.

The alcohol. The cigarettes. The unavailable men. The numbing.

It all fell away when I finally became available to myself, maybe for the first time.

I'm sharing this because I know what it feels like to live in a body that never feels quite like home.
And I know there's another way.

Because the quality of your life isn't only shaped by what happens to you.

It's shaped by how you are with yourself while it does.
