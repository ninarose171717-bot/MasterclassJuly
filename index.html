About Me

I've spent the last three years working with women, online and in person, around one pattern that sits beneath so much of what keeps us stuck: the habit of leaving ourselves.

I also work in rehab with people in recovery. Different circumstances, same question: how do we stay with ourselves when life becomes difficult?

My work is grounded in the body, because real change doesn't happen through insight alone. But more than any method or modality, what I bring is a greater capacity to be present with what's difficult, without rushing to fix it, change it, numb it, or look away.

I'm originally from Slovenia and now live in Thailand. Choosing a life that felt true rather than one that looked right has shaped everything about the work I do.

For years, I believed that if I could just heal enough, understand myself enough, figure out the right thing, I would finally feel at peace.

But peace kept slipping away. Instead, I found myself reaching for everything outside of me to fill something I couldn't name.

I was obsessed with men who weren't available to me. I drank every day just to function in social settings. I could only be emotionally open when I was using something. I outsourced my sense of safety to everything outside of me.

I tried so many things, mostly at once. Committing only to quit after a few days, fighting myself and the cravings.

Nothing worked.

What changed wasn't a method. It wasn't a modality.

I learned, slowly, gently, and with a lot of love, how to actually be with myself. Not my emotions. Myself.

And somewhere in that process, I stopped needing what I was reaching for.

The alcohol. The cigarettes. The unavailable men. The sugar, the numbing.

Not through force. Through finally becoming available to myself, maybe for the first time.

I'm sharing this because I know what it feels like to live in a body that never feels quite like home.

And I know there's another way.

Because the quality of your life isn't only shaped by what happens to you.

It's shaped by how you are with yourself while it does.
